Lakásbiztosítás

Lakásbiztosítás is Hungarian home insurance — a policy covering the structure and contents of a residential property against damage, loss, and liability risks.

While not legally mandatory for outright buyers in Hungary, lakásbiztosítás (home insurance) is universally required by mortgage lenders as a condition of their jelzálog. The lender is typically named as a co-beneficiary on the policy up to the outstanding loan amount.

Hungarian home insurance policies typically include two main components: épületbiztosítás (building/structure insurance) covering the fabric of the property, and ingóságbiztosítás (contents insurance) covering movable possessions. Many providers offer bundled packages with liability cover (felelősségbiztosítás) included.

Annual premiums in Hungary vary widely depending on the property's location, value, age, and construction type. Properties in flood risk zones or areas prone to subsidence attract higher premiums. The Hungarian insurance regulator (MNB) requires all insurers operating in Hungary to be licensed.

When purchasing a property with an existing lakásbiztosítás policy, buyers must arrange their own policy from the date of completion — the seller's policy does not transfer. Buyers should arrange cover to start on completion day to avoid any gap.

Common Mistake: Assuming the purchase of a Hungarian property includes the seller's insurance. It does not — Hungarian insurance policies are personal to the policyholder and must be cancelled or amended by the seller at completion.
Expert Tip: Choose a sum insured based on the reconstruction cost (újjáépítési érték) of the building, not the purchase price. In many Hungarian markets, especially outside Budapest, these figures differ significantly.

Comparing lakásbiztosítás quotes online is straightforward — major Hungarian insurers including Allianz, Generali, K&H, and Groupama offer online calculators. International buyers should confirm that policies cover natural disaster risks relevant to their property's location.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is home insurance mandatory in Hungary?

Lakásbiztosítás is not legally mandatory for outright buyers in Hungary, but all mortgage lenders require it as a condition of the jelzálog. In practice, almost all financed purchases include a policy.

What does Hungarian home insurance typically cover?

A standard lakásbiztosítás covers the building structure (fire, storm, flood, subsidence), contents (theft, accidental damage), and often third-party liability. Exclusions vary by policy — always read the terms carefully.

Who regulates Hungarian insurance companies?

The Magyar Nemzeti Bank (MNB — National Bank of Hungary) acts as the financial regulator and supervises all insurance companies operating in Hungary, including those offering lakásbiztosítás products.
