CSOK

CSOK (Családi Otthonteremtési Kedvezmény) is the Hungarian Family Home Creation Allowance — a government grant and preferential loan programme supporting families with children to purchase or build a home.

CSOK is one of Hungary's most significant housing policy tools, introduced in 2015 and periodically expanded. It provides non-repayable grants to families based on the number of existing or planned children, combined with access to a preferential fixed-rate mortgage at below-market interest rates.

The programme applies to both new-build and used property purchases, though grant amounts differ. For new builds, families with three or more children can receive a grant of HUF 10 million or more. For used properties, the amounts are lower. The preferential loan (CSOK-hitel) is capped at 3% interest and offered by participating banks.

CSOK eligibility requires Hungarian citizenship, a clean Hungarian tax record, and a minimum period of National Health Insurance (TB) coverage. Applicants must not have previously defaulted on a government loan. The programme is administered through the commercial banking system.

From 2024, the programme was expanded under the 'Otthon Start' initiative with increased grant amounts and extended eligibility windows. The terms have changed multiple times since 2015, so buyers should verify current conditions directly with participating banks or a CSOK specialist.

Common Mistake: Relying on outdated CSOK parameters. The programme's rules, amounts, and eligibility criteria change frequently with government budget cycles. Always verify current terms at a participating bank before assuming eligibility.
Expert Tip: Apply for CSOK pre-approval before committing to a property purchase. The approval process can take several weeks and requires extensive documentation — starting early avoids delays that could jeopardise the transaction timeline.

CSOK is only available for primary residences — it cannot be used for investment properties or holiday homes. The purchased property must remain the family's primary address for at least 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners apply for CSOK in Hungary?

CSOK is available to Hungarian citizens. EU nationals who have established Hungarian residency and meet the TB contribution requirement may also qualify. Non-EU nationals are generally not eligible.

Is the CSOK grant repayable in Hungary?

The CSOK grant is non-repayable as long as the conditions are met — primarily maintaining the property as the family's primary residence for 10 years. Failure to meet conditions triggers repayment with interest.

What is the difference between the CSOK grant and the CSOK loan?

The CSOK grant is a non-repayable subsidy from the Hungarian state. The CSOK loan (CSOK-hitel) is a preferential fixed-rate mortgage offered by participating banks at a subsidised interest rate of up to 3% per annum, separate from the grant.
