TL;DR — Budapest District Buying Guide

For lifestyle buyers: Districts I, II, V, XII offer prestige but lowest yields (3–4%). For investor buyers: Districts VII, VIII, IX, XIII, XIX offer 5–8% gross yields with lower entry costs. Always check condominium house rules before buying for short-let — Budapest's V–VII districts have the strictest restrictions. Heritage property in Districts I, II, and III needs NÖSZTÉP approval for structural works.

Frequently Asked Questions — Buying in Budapest

Can foreigners rent out Budapest apartments on Airbnb?
Budapest introduced short-let restrictions under District V Municipal Decree 44/2022 and national Government Decree 235/2019. In the inner districts (V–VII especially), condominium house rules (házirend) frequently prohibit short-let use. Violations can result in fines of HUF 500,000–1,000,000 and condominium association legal action. Your lawyer must check: (1) the SZÉP card / turisztikai szálláshely licence requirement; (2) the condominium house rules; (3) any district-level permit requirement — before you sign.
What is elővásárlási jog and does it affect my Budapest purchase?
Elővásárlási jog (pre-emption right) is the right of a third party to purchase a property ahead of you at the same price. It can be held by: (1) the municipality (önkormányzat) — especially in inner-city renewal zones and historical buildings; (2) co-owners of jointly-owned property; (3) tenants under Act LXXXIX of 2021. Before signing, your lawyer checks the title deed (tulajdoni lap) for registered elővásárlási jog entries and contacts the municipality under Act V of 2013 (Ptk.) § 6:222. Failure to notify a pre-emption right holder makes the sale voidable.
What is a VEFA purchase and what are the risks for off-plan buyers?
VEFA (Vételár Előre Fizetés Alapján — off-plan purchase with staged payments) is how most Budapest new-build apartments are sold. Risks include: developer insolvency before completion; specification changes; delayed handover. Protection under Act V of 2013 (Ptk.) § 6:213 requires a bank guarantee (bankgarancia) or insurance guarantee from the developer covering staged payments. Your lawyer must verify this guarantee is in place before any payment. District XIII and IX are currently the busiest new-build markets.
How long does a Budapest property purchase take from offer to title registration?
A standard resale purchase follows this timeline: Preliminary contract (előszerződés) signing: Day 1. Lawyer due diligence (title, encumbrances, condominium rules): 3–7 days. Final contract (adásvételi szerződés) signing: Day 10–14. Land registry (Ingatlan-nyilvántartás) filing: Within 30 days of signing (mandatory). Land registry registration completion: 30–90 days (standard); 15 days (urgent — HUF 10,000 priority fee). Practical occupation: Usually 30–45 days from signing. The title does not pass to you until the land registry registers the transfer — your lawyer files a lien (jelzálogjog) to protect your position in the interim.
📋 Case Study

American buyer purchases District VII apartment for Airbnb — and why they almost couldn't

Purchase price: HUF 78M (2-bed, 85m², ruin bar district, heritage building)

Intended use: Short-let tourist apartment

Issue discovered during due diligence: Condominium house rules prohibited short-term lettings of under 30 days. Additionally, the building (pre-1945, heritage-listed) would require NÖSZTÉP approval for any renovation works.

Resolution: Buyer renegotiated with seller for a 10% price reduction (HUF 7.8M) reflecting the use restriction. Property retained as a long-term let instead. Annual rent: HUF 4,200,000 (5.4% gross yield).

Lawyer fee: HUF 520,000. Stamp duty: HUF 3,120,000. NÖSZTÉP heritage survey: HUF 180,000.

Legal basis: Act LXIV of 2001 (heritage); Government Decree 235/2019 (short-let registration); Act CXCVII of 2016 (condominium/társasház law) §§ 31–35; Act XCIII of 1990 (illeték).
