Jelzálog

Jelzálog is the Hungarian term for a mortgage or charge over real property, registering the lender's security interest in the Földhivatal to secure repayment of a loan.

In Hungary, a jelzálog (mortgage) is established by a notarial deed or lawyer-countersigned contract and registered in the Földhivatal's Section III of the tulajdoni lap. The registered jelzálog gives the lender priority over the property in the event of default.

Hungarian mortgage law permits both bank mortgages (for home loan financing) and private mortgages (between individuals as security for personal loans). The priority of competing jelzálogok is determined by order of registration — earlier registration takes precedence.

When buying a property with an existing jelzálog, the purchase price is typically structured so that the seller's mortgage is discharged from the proceeds at closing — the buyer's lawyer coordinates the simultaneous repayment and deregistration. Buyers should never accept a property with an undischarged jelzálog unless this is explicitly negotiated.

Hungarian banks commonly require a valuation (értékbecslés) before approving a jelzálog. The loan-to-value ratio is regulated — lenders cannot advance more than 80% of the property's appraised value for primary residences (70% for second homes and non-residents).

Common Mistake: Assuming a seller's verbal assurance that a jelzálog 'will be paid off' at closing is sufficient. Always verify in writing, include a completion condition in the adásvételi szerződés, and ensure the discharge is coordinated simultaneously with fund release.
Expert Tip: For Hungarian home purchases involving a jelzálog, ask your bank for a jelzálog-törlési nyilatkozat (mortgage release letter) conditional on receipt of the payoff amount — this protects the transaction timeline.

After repaying a Hungarian mortgage, promptly instruct your lawyer to obtain the lender's törlési engedély (deregistration consent) and file for removal from the Földhivatal — a discharged but still-registered jelzálog complicates any future sale.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is a jelzálog removed from the Hungarian land register?

Once the loan is repaid, the lender issues a törlési engedély (consent to deregister). This document is filed with the Földhivatal by the borrower's lawyer, and the jelzálog notation is removed from Section III of the tulajdoni lap.

Can a foreign bank hold a jelzálog on Hungarian property?

Yes, subject to Hungarian legal requirements for registration. The jelzálog must be established in accordance with Hungarian law and registered in the Földhivatal to be effective as security against third parties.

What is the maximum LTV ratio for a jelzálog in Hungary?

Under Hungarian National Bank regulations, lenders may advance up to 80% of appraised value for primary residences of Hungarian residents, and up to 70% for second homes or non-resident buyers.
