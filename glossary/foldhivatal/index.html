Földhivatal

The Földhivatal is the Hungarian Land Registry Office responsible for recording all real property ownership, encumbrances, and easements in the Hungarian property register (ingatlan-nyilvántartás).

The Földhivatal maintains the ingatlan-nyilvántartás — Hungary's official land register. Every piece of real property in Hungary has a unique entry in this register identified by its helyrajzi szám (lot number). Ownership only legally transfers upon registration in the Földhivatal.

The Földhivatal is operated under the supervision of the Ministry of Justice. County-level offices handle registrations for properties within their jurisdiction. The online portal (takarnet.hu) allows professionals to search the register; a simplified public extract (e-TET) is available to the general public.

Upon submission of a valid adásvételi szerződés, the Földhivatal places a széljegy (notation) on the property's entry, alerting any subsequent searcher that a registration is pending. This protects the buyer against the seller attempting a double sale during the registration period.

The tulajdoni lap (title extract) has three sections: Section I describes the property; Section II shows current and historical owners; Section III records all encumbrances such as mortgages (jelzálog), easements (szolgalom), and judicial attachments (végrehajtási jog). Section III must be clear before purchase.

Common Mistake: Relying on an old tulajdoni lap. Encumbrances can be registered quickly, so a document more than a day old may be misleading. Always pull a fresh extract on the day of signing.
Expert Tip: Ask your ügyvéd to run a simultaneous search at the Földhivatal on the day of signing and confirm no széljegy from another transaction is already on the property.

Always obtain a fresh tulajdoni lap immediately before signing — Hungarian lawyers typically pull this electronically minutes before the signing appointment to catch any last-minute encumbrances.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a tulajdoni lap in Hungary?

A tulajdoni lap is the official title extract from the Földhivatal, divided into three sections covering property description, ownership history, and encumbrances. It is the definitive document for due diligence in any Hungarian property transaction.

What does széljegy mean in Hungarian property law?

A széljegy is an annotation placed on a property's Földhivatal entry upon receipt of a registration application, signalling a pending change. It protects the incoming buyer from a competing registration during the processing period.

Can foreigners buy property in Hungary?

EU citizens can generally purchase Hungarian property on the same terms as Hungarian nationals. Non-EU foreign nationals require a permit from the local government (járási hivatal). Agricultural land has additional restrictions.
