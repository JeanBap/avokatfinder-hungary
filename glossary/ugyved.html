Ügyvéd (Hungarian Lawyer)

A licensed legal professional in Hungary authorised to represent clients in legal matters, draft contracts, and authenticate real estate transactions.

An ügyvéd (lawyer or attorney) is a registered member of the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara (Hungarian Bar Association) authorised to practise law in Hungary. Unlike some European countries where notaries handle most private transactions, Hungarian law gives ügyvédek a central role in property deals, company formations, and other legal transactions. They are the only professionals authorised to authenticate property transfer contracts and represent clients before the Földhivatal. Without a countersignature from an ügyvéd, a property sale contract is not valid for registration purposes.

Hungarian ügyvédek provide a wide range of legal services including property conveyancing, employment law advice, company registration, civil litigation, criminal defence, and family law matters. For expatriates, finding an ügyvéd who speaks English or another major European language is often essential. Many Hungarian law firms in Budapest and other major cities have international desks specifically serving foreign clients. Verify that any lawyer you engage is registered with the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara before proceeding with any legal matter.

The fees charged by ügyvédek in Hungary are not regulated by a fixed tariff for most work, though property conveyancing fees typically range from 0.5% to 1% of the property purchase price, with a minimum fee. This fee covers the preparation and authentication of the purchase contract, identity verification, Földhivatal registration application, and general coordination of the transaction. Always agree the fee scope in writing before engaging a lawyer. Additional costs such as Földhivatal registration fees, stamp duties, and translation costs are usually charged separately.

For property transactions, your ügyvéd acts as the safeguard between buyer and seller. They will review the title deed, check for encumbrances and annotations, verify the seller has authority to sell, prepare the contract, and manage the registration process. Many ügyvédek also manage the payment escrow, holding the purchase price until registration is confirmed. This escrow service (bizalmi őrzés) reduces risk for buyers but comes with an additional fee. Always clarify whether your ügyvéd is offering this service or whether you need to arrange payment timing separately.

Hungarian lawyers specialising in company law are also essential for business formation. Setting up a Korlátolt Felelősségű Társaság (Kft., the Hungarian equivalent of a private limited company) requires an ügyvéd to prepare the articles of association and register the company with the court of registration. The lawyer must authenticate the founding documents and submit the registration application. Company formation is typically completed within a few business days if documents are in order. Annual legal maintenance of a Hungarian company also often requires periodic involvement from an ügyvéd.

Key Facts

  • Must be registered with Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara (Hungarian Bar Association)
  • Only ügyvédek can authenticate property transfer contracts for Földhivatal registration
  • Property conveyancing fees typically 0.5%-1% of purchase price
  • Can provide escrow (bizalmi őrzés) services to secure purchase funds
  • Essential for company formation and registration of Hungarian entities

⚠ Common Mistake

Foreign buyers sometimes use their home country lawyer to draft a Hungarian property contract, thinking it saves money. Hungarian property law requires that contracts be countersigned by a Hungarian-registered ügyvéd to be valid for registration—no foreign lawyer can substitute for this requirement. Additionally, some buyers engage an ügyvéd recommended by the seller's estate agent, creating a potential conflict of interest. Always engage your own independent lawyer who represents only your interests.

💡 Practical Tip

When interviewing potential ügyvédek, ask specifically about their experience with international clients and whether they have conducted transactions for foreign buyers from your home country. Ask for references. Verify their registration at the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara website before engaging them. A good property lawyer will proactively flag any issues in the title sheet and advise on pre-emption rights before you sign.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use any lawyer in Hungary for a property purchase?

You can use any ügyvéd registered with the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara, but it is strongly recommended to choose one with specific property conveyancing experience and preferably with international client experience. Avoid using the same lawyer as the seller or one recommended solely by the estate agent, as conflicts of interest may arise.

How much does a Hungarian lawyer cost for property conveyancing?

Expect fees of 0.5% to 1% of the purchase price, with minimum fees often around HUF 100,000-200,000 for smaller properties. Additional costs such as land registry fees, duty stamps, and expedited registration fees are charged on top. Always request a written fee estimate before engagement.

Is a Hungarian notary (közjegyző) the same as an ügyvéd?

No. A közjegyző (notary) is a public official who handles specific functions such as estate administration, authentication of documents, and enforcement orders. An ügyvéd is a private lawyer who represents client interests. For property transactions, you need an ügyvéd to countersign the contract, though a közjegyző may be involved in other aspects of the process.
