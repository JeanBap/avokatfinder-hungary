⚠ Hungary's "National Card" scheme (Nemzeti Kártya) is a bilateral work permit track for nationals of Ukraine, Russia, Serbia, Belarus, North Macedonia, Mongolia, and Kyrgyzstan — allowing faster access than standard work permits. Key legislation: Act II of 2007 (third-country nationals); Act I of 2007 (EU nationals); Act LV of 1993 (citizenship).

TL;DR — Quick Answer

Most non-EU nationals need both a work permit and a residence permit to live and work in Hungary. The combined process takes 4–12 weeks. EU nationals and their family members only need to register — not apply for a permit. Lawyer representation is not mandatory but significantly reduces rejection risk.

📋 Case Study

Indian software engineer relocates to Budapest tech company

Scenario: Bangalore-based developer offered senior role at Budapest fintech. Employer has EU Blue Card sponsorship capacity.

Route chosen: EU Blue Card (Kék Kártya) — requires minimum annual salary of €20,352 (2025 threshold for ICT sector) and University degree.

Timeline: Employment contract signed → visa application (Indian embassy Budapest) → OIF residence permit → Social Security registration (TAJ number) → Tax ID (adószám): Total: 9 weeks

Lawyer cost (immigration specialist): HUF 380,000

OIF fees: HUF 6,000 (permit) + HUF 3,000 (biometric card)

Family reunification (spouse + 1 child added 6 weeks later): HUF 180,000 additional lawyer fee

Legal basis: Act II of 2007 §§ 20–32 (EU Blue Card); Council Directive 2009/50/EC (EU Blue Card Directive); Act LXXX of 1997 (social insurance); Government Decree 146/2022 (Blue Card salary thresholds).
