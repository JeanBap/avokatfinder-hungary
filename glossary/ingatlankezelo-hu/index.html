Ingatlankezelo (Property Management Company)

An ingatlankezelo is a professional property management company in Hungary that handles tenant relations, maintenance, accounts, and legal compliance on behalf of landlords — increasingly common as the Budapest rental market professionalises.

Hungary's rental market — particularly Budapest — has grown rapidly, attracting both domestic and foreign investors. As portfolios expand, many landlords engage ingatlankezelo firms to manage day-to-day operations, tenant screening, rent collection, and maintenance coordination.

An ingatlankezelo typically charges 8–12% of gross monthly rent as a management fee. Services usually include advertising vacancies, vetting tenants (credit checks, references), drafting lease agreements, collecting rent, handling minor repairs, and liaising with the housing community (lakasszovetseg) on behalf of the owner.

Hungarian tenancy law (Act LXXVIII of 1993) is relatively landlord-friendly. Fixed-term leases automatically end at expiry without notice. However, once a tenancy becomes indefinite (hatarozatlan idotar) the landlord must provide longer notice periods and follow statutory eviction procedures if the tenant fails to leave.

For short-term lets (Airbnb and similar), Budapest introduced local authority registration requirements in 2022. Property managers can handle the registration and tax obligations — hosts must pay a tourist tax (idegenforgalmi ado) per guest-night and declare income as a self-employed activity.

When selecting an ingatlankezelo, verify that the firm holds a real estate agency licence (ingatlankozvetitoi muengedely) issued by the government, as property management falls under the same regulatory framework as agency work.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Paying a management fee without a written management agreement leaves you with no recourse if the firm mismanages funds or fails to pass on rental income. Always insist on a signed contract.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For a Budapest rental property, ensure your ingatlankezelo is familiar with the specific district's short-term let rules — requirements vary by kerület (district) and change frequently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an ingatlankezelo if I have just one rental property?

Not necessarily, but if you live abroad or have a demanding primary occupation, the fee is usually recovered in time saved and better-maintained tenant relationships.

Can a foreign owner use an ingatlankezelo to manage a Hungarian property?

Yes, and it is common. The management company can also assist with opening a Hungarian bank account for rental income and handling local tax filings on your behalf.

What taxes apply to rental income in Hungary?

Rental income is subject to 15% SZJA. Landlords can deduct the standard 10% cost allowance or actual documented costs. Income must be declared in the annual tax return.
