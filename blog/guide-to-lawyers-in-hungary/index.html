The Complete Guide to Finding a Lawyer in Hungary as an Expat

Everything expats and foreign nationals need to know about the Hungarian legal system, law firm types, legal costs, and how to find the right English-speaking lawyer for your situation.

📖 20 min read Updated 2026 🇭🇺 Hungary

Hungary has a well-developed legal system rooted in continental European law. For expats and foreign nationals, navigating this system without the right lawyer can be costly and confusing. This guide explains how the Hungarian legal system works, how to find the right English-speaking lawyer, and what to expect when you first meet with a Hungarian attorney.

How the Hungarian Legal System Works

Hungary operates a civil law system influenced by Austrian and German legal traditions. The Hungarian Bar Association (Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara) regulates all licensed attorneys (ügyvédek). Courts are organised in a four-tier structure: district courts (járásbíróságok), regional courts (törvényszékek), courts of appeal (ítélőtáblák) and the Supreme Court (Kúria).

Hungary is an EU member state, meaning EU law applies directly and international businesses can rely on harmonised rules in many areas. Dispute resolution in Hungary often involves the Budapest Arbitration Centre (BAC) for commercial matters.

Types of Law Firms in Hungary

The Hungarian legal market features three main categories of firms. Large international firms such as CMS, Baker McKenzie, DLA Piper and Kinstellar handle high-value cross-border transactions, M&A and capital markets. Leading domestic independents such as Lakatos, Köves & Partners, Szecskay Attorneys, Oppenheim and Jalsovszky serve both international and domestic clients across a full range of business law matters. Boutique and specialist firms, such as Süle Law (IP), ZHK Law (family law) and Wagner & Wagner (immigration), provide deep expertise in specific areas and often have lower fee rates than the large firms.

How to Find an English-Speaking Lawyer in Hungary

The most reliable sources for verified English-speaking lawyers in Hungary are Chambers Europe (published annually, available at chambers.com), the Legal 500 EMEA guide (legal500.com/c/hungary) and IFLR1000 for financial law firms. The US Embassy in Budapest also maintains a list of English-speaking attorneys at its website. AvökatFinder's Hungary directory is built from these verified sources.

What to Expect in an Initial Consultation

Most Hungarian law firms offer a 30–60 minute initial consultation. You should bring any relevant documents translated into English or Hungarian. The lawyer will assess your situation, explain the legal framework that applies and give an indication of costs. Ask whether they handle your specific type of matter regularly, and whether they have English-language client communications as a standard practice — not just a courtesy.

Legal Costs in Hungary

Hourly rates for senior partners at top international firms in Budapest range from €250 to €500. Mid-size domestic firms typically charge €100–€250/hr. Boutique and specialist firms often offer fixed fees for defined matters such as immigration applications (typically €500–€1,500), property purchase (typically 1% of purchase price) and divorce (€800–€2,500 depending on complexity). Always request a written fee agreement.

What Documents You Need

For most matters you will need: passport and valid Hungarian address documentation, any existing contracts or legal documents relevant to your matter, relevant correspondence with Hungarian authorities, and any prior legal documents from your home country if relevant. Immigration matters additionally require employment contracts, proof of accommodation and financial sufficiency documentation.

Legal Aid in Hungary

Hungary provides a legal aid system for qualifying individuals. Applications are made through the regional Legal Aid Service (Igazságügyi Szolgálat). EU citizens may also access legal assistance through cross-border legal aid under EU Directive 2003/8/EC.
