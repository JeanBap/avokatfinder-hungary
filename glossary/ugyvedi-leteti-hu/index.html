Ugyvedi Leteti Szamla (Lawyer's Escrow Account)

In Hungarian property transactions, the ugyvedi leteti szamla is the lawyer's client escrow account that holds the purchase price securely between signing and registration, protecting both buyer and seller.

Unlike many European countries where notaries hold transaction funds, Hungary routes purchase monies through the conveyancing lawyer's escrow account (ugyvedi leteti szamla). The buyer transfers the full purchase price to this account before or on the day of signing. The lawyer holds the funds and releases them to the seller only after the land registry confirms the buyer's ownership.

This mechanism protects the buyer from losing the purchase price if the transaction collapses post-signing and before registration. It also protects the seller from signing over title before receiving funds. The lawyer acts as a neutral custodian and is bound by bar association rules and client account regulations.

The lawyer must maintain a separate client account for each transaction and cannot commingle funds with their own money. Interest earned on the escrow — typically negligible — may be retained by the lawyer unless otherwise agreed.

Mortgage funds from the buyer's bank are also routed through the escrow account. The bank releases the mortgage to the escrow once it receives confirmation that the property registration application has been filed with the land registry.

If any encumbrance needs to be cleared from the title — such as the seller's existing mortgage — the lawyer uses funds from escrow to pay off the seller's lender directly before releasing the remainder to the seller. This ensures clean title passes to the buyer.

Key Facts

Who holds fundsConveyancing lawyer (not notary, not bank)
When releasedAfter land registry confirms buyer's registration
ProtectionBuyer protected from pre-registration loss
MortgageBank releases to escrow on filing confirmation
Encumbrance payoffLawyer pays seller's lender directly from escrow

Common Mistake

Watch out: Never transfer purchase funds directly to the seller before registration. In Hungary, all funds must go through the lawyer's escrow account — any seller requesting direct payment should be treated as a red flag.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your lawyer for the specific escrow account number in writing and verify it independently (call the bar association) before transferring any funds. Account fraud targeting property transactions is a known risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the transaction fails after I've paid into escrow?

The lawyer holds the funds and returns them to you if the transaction does not complete, subject to any agreed deposit or penalty provisions in the contract.

Can I use the escrow account for a mortgage transaction?

Yes, and it is standard practice. Your bank will require confirmation that the land registry application has been filed before releasing mortgage funds, which the lawyer provides.

Is the lawyer's escrow account insured?

Lawyers in Hungary are required to hold professional indemnity insurance. However, in the event of fraud or misappropriation by the lawyer, recovery can be complex — always verify the lawyer's registration with the Hungarian Bar Association.
