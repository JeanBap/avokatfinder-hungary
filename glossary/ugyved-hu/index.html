Ügyvéd

An ügyvéd is a Hungarian lawyer — a licensed legal professional whose countersignature is legally required on all real estate purchase contracts for them to be valid for land registry submission.

The mandatory involvement of an ügyvéd in Hungarian property transactions is a fundamental feature of the legal system. Unlike many countries where notaries handle property transfers, Hungary places this role with registered lawyers. The ügyvéd drafts, explains, and countersigns the adásvételi szerződés.

An ügyvéd must be registered with the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara (Hungarian Bar Association) and carry professional indemnity insurance. Their fee is typically 0.5% to 1% of the purchase price, though this varies. Both buyer and seller may use the same ügyvéd, but separate representation is advisable for complex transactions.

The ügyvéd is responsible for verifying the Földhivatal extract, identifying encumbrances, checking the seller's identity, drafting the contract to protect the buyer's interests, countersigning it, and submitting it to the Földhivatal within 30 days of signing.

For foreign buyers, the ügyvéd also plays an important advisory role regarding Hungarian property ownership restrictions, tax implications, and the requirements of the National Bank of Hungary for currency conversion if the purchase price is in a foreign currency.

Common Mistake: Using the seller's ügyvéd to represent both parties. While legally possible with consent, conflicts of interest are common — particularly on price negotiations, completion conditions, and penalty clauses.
Expert Tip: Ask the ügyvéd for a draft of the contract 48 hours before the signing appointment so you have time to review and ask questions. Rushing at the signing table leads to overlooked terms.

Engaging a bilingual ügyvéd fluent in both Hungarian and the buyer's language is strongly advisable for international buyers to ensure full understanding of all contractual terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is a lawyer required for Hungarian property purchases?

Hungarian law mandates that an adásvételi szerződés for real property must bear an ügyvéd's countersignature to be accepted by the Földhivatal. This ensures legal validity, proper identity verification, and professional oversight of the transaction.

How do I verify a Hungarian lawyer's registration?

Check the Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara's online directory at portal.mük.hu. Registered ügyvédek are searchable by name, and you can confirm their active status and regional bar membership.

How much does an ügyvéd charge for a Hungarian property transaction?

Fees vary but typically range from 0.5% to 1% of the purchase price, often with a minimum charge of around HUF 100,000–200,000. Some law firms offer fixed fees for standard residential transactions.
