Situational Guide

What to Do in a Legal Emergency in Hungary: Who to Call

Your house is being repossessed. You're getting divorced. You've been arrested. You've been scammed. A practical guide to the right lawyer for each crisis situation in Hungary.

📖 15 min read Updated 2026 🇭🇺 Hungary

Legal emergencies happen when you least expect them. This guide walks through the most common legal crisis situations that expats and foreigners face in Hungary — and tells you exactly what to do and which type of lawyer to call.

1. Your Property is Being Repossessed or You Have a Dispute With a Landlord

If you receive a notice threatening eviction, a bank foreclosure or a property dispute, call a real estate and dispute resolution lawyer immediately. In Hungary, enforcement proceedings (végrehajtás) move quickly once initiated. A specialist like CMS, Kinstellar, Szecskay or a boutique firm such as Szécsényi & Partners can apply for an injunction (végrehajtás felfüggesztése) to pause proceedings. Act within days, not weeks.

Who to call: Real Estate Lawyers in Hungary · Litigation Lawyers

2. You Are Getting Divorced

Divorce in Hungary (válóper) is filed at the district court. For expats, the key complication is often cross-border elements: children of different nationalities, assets in multiple countries, or a spouse who leaves Hungary. Specialist firms including ZHK Law (zhklaw.hu), Studio Legale (studiolegale.hu) and Kovács Hawa are experienced in handling international divorces. Child custody matters involving a Hungarian and foreign parent can escalate into Hague Convention proceedings — do not delay getting specialist advice.

Who to call: Family Law Lawyers in Hungary

3. Someone Has Hit You — How Do You File a Lawsuit?

Personal injury and assault cases in Hungary can proceed both criminally (through the police, rendőrség) and civilly (for damages). File a police report first. If you want compensation beyond what criminal proceedings provide, you need a civil litigation lawyer to file a tort claim (kártérítési per) in district court. DLA Piper, Szecskay, Kapolyi and Nagy és Trócsányi are all strong dispute resolution firms.

Who to call: Dispute Resolution Lawyers

4. You Have Been Arrested or Detained

If you are arrested in Hungary, you have the right to a lawyer immediately. The police must inform you of the charges and allow you to contact an attorney. Contact your embassy or consulate as soon as possible — they can provide a list of English-speaking criminal defence lawyers. Kecser Law Firm (kecser.com) and specialist criminal defence practices can provide urgent representation. Do not answer police questions without a lawyer present.

Who to call: Your embassy · Criminal Defence Lawyers

5. You Need a Work Permit or Residence Permit Urgently

Hungarian immigration law changed significantly in 2024 with the introduction of the Guest Worker scheme and new permit categories. If your permit has expired or you have been issued a departure order, you need a specialist immigration lawyer immediately. Wagner & Wagner (wagnerlaw.hu, +36 1 337 6518), Kecser Law, Helpers Hungary and Immigration Lawyers Hungary (+36 20 377 1030) all handle urgent permit matters. The National Directorate-General for Aliens Policing (OIF) is the competent authority — your lawyer will correspond directly with OIF on your behalf.

Who to call: Immigration Lawyers in Hungary

6. Your Employer Is Treating You Unfairly

Hungarian employment law (Munka Törvénykönyve, 2012) provides strong protections against unfair dismissal, discrimination and non-payment of wages. If you believe you have been wrongfully dismissed or harassed, you have 30 days to file a claim with the Labour Court (Munkaügyi Bíróság). Baker McKenzie, Forgó Damjanovic & Partners and Szecskay all have strong employment law teams with English-speaking capability.

Who to call: Employment Lawyers in Hungary

7. You Are Buying Property and Something Has Gone Wrong

Property purchases in Hungary require a lawyer — it is not optional. If your conveyancing has gone wrong (title issues, undisclosed debts, boundary disputes), you need a specialist real estate litigation lawyer. Ecovis Zalavári Legal, Kovács Hawa and Szécsényi & Partners can investigate title issues. For high-value disputes, Dentons or Kinstellar have the resources to handle complex property litigation.

Who to call: Real Estate Lawyers

8. You Have Been Scammed or Defrauded

If you have been the victim of fraud in Hungary, you should file a criminal complaint with the police (rendőrség) and simultaneously instruct a civil litigation lawyer to pursue a damages claim. White-collar fraud cases require specialists with both criminal and civil law capability. Oppenheim and Nagy és Trócsányi have experience in complex fraud and financial disputes.

Who to call: Police (+36 1 443 5600) · Dispute Resolution Lawyers

9. Your Company is in Trouble

If your Hungarian company faces insolvency (felszámolás), creditor actions or a regulatory investigation, you need immediate advice from an experienced corporate or insolvency lawyer. Oppenheim, KCG Partners and Lakatos, Köves & Partners all handle corporate restructuring and insolvency matters. The Hungarian insolvency regime follows EU standards, with appointed liquidators (felszámoló) managing insolvent estates.

Who to call: Corporate Lawyers in Hungary

10. Someone Has Died and There Is an Inheritance Dispute

Hungarian inheritance law is governed by the Civil Code and follow the EU Succession Regulation for cross-border cases. If you are named in a Hungarian will, have assets in Hungary, or have a dispute with other heirs, a lawyer can represent you before the notary (közjegyző) who handles the estate procedure. Dr. Pécsváradi Luca Anna and Kovács Hawa regularly assist foreign nationals in Hungarian inheritance matters.

Who to call: Family and Inheritance Lawyers
