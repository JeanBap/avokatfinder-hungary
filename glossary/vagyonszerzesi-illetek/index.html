Vagyonszerzési Illeték

Vagyonszerzési illeték is the Hungarian property acquisition duty — a transfer tax of 4% of the property's market value paid by the buyer upon purchasing real estate in Hungary.

Vagyonszerzési illeték (property acquisition duty) is payable by the buyer on the purchase price or the market value of the property, whichever is higher. The standard rate is 4%, making it one of the significant transaction costs in Hungarian real estate purchases.

The illeték is assessed by the Hungarian Tax Authority (NAV — Nemzeti Adó- és Vámhivatal) after the adásvételi szerződés is submitted to the Földhivatal, which forwards a copy to NAV. The buyer receives an assessment notice and typically has 30 days to pay.

First-time buyers under 35 years old who are purchasing a property valued under HUF 15 million may pay the illeték in instalments over 12 months interest-free. This relief is administered by NAV automatically upon submission of the relevant declaration.

If NAV believes the declared purchase price is below market value, it can revalue the property and charge illeték on the higher assessed amount. The buyer has the right to challenge this assessment. Buyers paying below-market prices (e.g. from family) should be aware of this risk.

Common Mistake: Assuming the illeték is assessed immediately at signing. NAV typically sends the assessment weeks or months after Földhivatal submission, so buyers should maintain liquidity for the payment rather than assuming it won't arrive.
Expert Tip: Declare the purchase price accurately in the adásvételi szerződés. NAV cross-checks against comparable sales data, and a suspiciously low price triggers a revaluation and potential penalties.

Budget for vagyonszerzési illeték separately from legal fees and any agent commission when calculating the total cost of a Hungarian property acquisition — it alone adds 4% to the transaction cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does vagyonszerzési illeték become payable in Hungary?

The illeték becomes payable after NAV issues its assessment notice, which typically arrives several weeks after the Földhivatal receives the purchase contract. Payment is due within 30 days of the assessment date.

Are there exemptions from vagyonszerzési illeték in Hungary?

Yes — transfers between spouses are exempt, as are certain government-subsidised housing transactions. First-time buyers under 35 may qualify for an interest-free instalment option rather than a full exemption.

Can NAV charge illeték on a value higher than the purchase price?

Yes. If NAV considers the declared price below market value, it may issue an assessed value and charge the 4% rate on that higher figure. Buyers can challenge this assessment within 15 days of receipt.
