⚠ Hungary uses the fizetési meghagyás (payment order) system for undisputed debts — faster and cheaper than full court proceedings. If contested, it automatically converts to a civil claim. Hungarian courts operate in Hungarian only; foreign parties require certified translation of all pleadings. Key legislation: Act CXXX of 2016 (Pp. — Code of Civil Procedure); Act CXLV of 2017 (Arbitration Act).

TL;DR — Quick Answer

For cross-border commercial disputes involving Hungarian parties, include an ICC or Vienna arbitration clause in your contract before signing. Hungarian courts operate entirely in Hungarian, proceedings can take 2–4 years, and enforcement of foreign judgments requires separate exequatur proceedings under Act XXVIII of 2017.

📋 Case Study

UK supplier vs Hungarian distributor: €180,000 payment dispute

Scenario: London-based manufacturer supplies goods to Budapest distributor. Distributor disputes quality and withholds payment of €180,000 (HUF 71,100,000 at 395 EUR/HUF).

Contract clause: "Disputes resolved by courts of England and Wales under English law."

Problem: Post-Brexit, English judgments are not automatically enforceable in Hungary under the Brussels I Regulation. Recognition requires exequatur under Act XXVIII of 2017 § 108.

Strategy adopted: Parallel Hungarian fizetési meghagyás for undisputed portion (HUF 20,000,000) while English proceedings ran for the main claim. Hungarian payment order converted to judgment in 6 months. English judgment recognised in Hungary 14 months later.

Total lawyer cost (Budapest + London dual instruction): HUF 9,800,000

Legal basis: Act CXXX of 2016 (Pp.) §§ 386–392; Act XXVIII of 2017 §§ 101–118 (PIL recognition); Act LV of 2009 (fizetési meghagyás); Brexit Trade and Cooperation Agreement Art. 782 (no mutual enforcement provisions).
