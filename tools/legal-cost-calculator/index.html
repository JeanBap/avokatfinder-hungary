Key Hungarian Company Law Facts

  • Legal basis: Act V of 2013 on the Civil Code (Ptk.) — company law chapters; Act V of 2006 on Company Registration and Publicity
  • Registered agent requirement: A Hungarian ügyvéd must countersign the deed of incorporation (alapítási okirat) and file with the Court of Registration (Cégbíróság)
  • Timeline: Simplified route: 1–3 business days. Standard: 5–15 business days. Complex (foreign parent, apostille required): 3–6 weeks
  • Foreign shareholders: No restriction — 100% foreign ownership permitted. Director must have a Hungarian tax ID (adóazonosító jel)
  • Annual accounts: Filed with Cégbíróság within 150 days of financial year-end. Accountant (könyvelő) required: HUF 20,000–80,000/month
⚠ Important: Share capital must be deposited into a Hungarian bank account before registration. The Cégbíróság (Court of Registration) processes filings electronically via the Hungarian Electronic Company System (e-Cégeljárás). Simplified formation uses model articles and cannot be customised; for any non-standard shareholding structure use the standard route.

What Drives Hungarian Lawyer Fees Up or Down

✓ Factors that REDUCE fees
  • Simple, standard transaction (model articles Kft., standard property purchase)
  • Existing client relationship (repeat business discount)
  • Documents already prepared / translated
  • Regional firm outside Budapest (20–40% cheaper)
  • Online / simplified procedure (e-Cégeljárás)
✗ Factors that INCREASE fees
  • Foreign party involvement (apostilles, translation, multi-jurisdiction)
  • Title defects or encumbrances on property
  • Urgency / expedited delivery
  • Contested proceedings (litigation, divorce)
  • High-value transaction (% fees on luxury property or M&A)
Source note: Fee ranges compiled from publicly disclosed fee structures, Hungarian Bar Association (Magyar Ügyvédi Kamara) guidance, and market surveys of English-speaking Budapest firms (2024–2025). Hungary abolished mandatory minimum fees under Act LXXXIV of 2007 as amended. VAT (ÁFA) at 27% applies to lawyer services for business clients.
