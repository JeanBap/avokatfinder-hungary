Illetekmentesseg (Stamp Duty Exemptions)

Illetekmentesseg covers the various exemptions from Hungary's 4% property acquisition duty, the most significant being the first-home benefit for buyers under 35 and the CSOK housing subsidy scheme.

Hungary levies an illetek (stamp duty / acquisition duty) of 4% on the fair market value of a second-hand property transfer. The most important exemption — and the one most buyers under 35 should investigate first — is the first-home exemption: purchasers buying their first residential property pay no duty at all, provided they have not previously owned a dwelling.

The CSOK (Otthonteremtesi Tamogatas) family housing subsidy scheme further interacts with stamp duty: properties purchased with CSOK are fully exempt from acquisition duty regardless of the buyer's age, as long as the property qualifies under the programme's rules.

Newly built properties purchased directly from a developer attract VAT (5% or 27% depending on the building permit date) rather than acquisition duty. Buyers should confirm which tax applies before budgeting.

Agricultural land transfers and inheritances in direct line are also exempt. For non-exempt transfers, duty is assessed by the National Tax and Customs Administration (NAV) based on its own valuation if the declared price is considered below market.

Stamp duty exemptions must be claimed at the time of filing the transfer notification with NAV — they cannot be reclaimed retroactively. Your lawyer will complete the notification form (B400) within 30 days of the deed of sale.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Failing to claim the first-home exemption on your B400 form means NAV will send an assessment for the full 4%. The exemption is not applied automatically — you must tick the box.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you are buying with a partner and only one qualifies for the exemption, ask your lawyer whether joint ownership can be structured to maximise the relief.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I claim stamp duty exemption on a second purchase?

No. The first-home exemption is a once-in-a-lifetime benefit. Once you have owned any residential property, even briefly, the exemption is gone.

What is the CSOK and how does it affect duty?

CSOK (now extended as CSOK Plus) is a state subsidy for families buying or building homes. Properties acquired using CSOK grants are automatically exempt from acquisition duty.

When does NAV send the stamp duty assessment?

Typically within 3–6 months of the B400 filing. You have the right to appeal NAV's valuation within 30 days of the assessment.
