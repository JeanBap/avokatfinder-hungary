Pécs Legal & Property Market Overview

Hungary's fifth-largest city, a UNESCO World Heritage site and cultural capital of Southern Transdanubia. Pécs has a large university population and strong cultural tourism. Its historic inner city (belváros) is protected under Act LXIV of 2001 (Cultural Heritage Protection) — restrictions apply to renovation and facade changes.

Frequently Asked Questions — Pécs

Can I renovate a historic property in Pécs?
Yes, but with restrictions. Properties in Pécs's historic core (Belváros) are listed under Act LXIV of 2001 on Cultural Heritage Protection. Structural changes require approval from the National Heritage Protection Office (NÖSZTÉP) and the local Heritage Protection Council. Your lawyer should commission a heritage impact assessment (örökségvédelmi hatástanulmány) before signing.
What are property prices in Pécs compared to Budapest?
Pécs properties sell for 40–55% less than equivalent Budapest stock. A 70m² apartment in the historic centre costs HUF 28–45M versus HUF 70–110M in Budapest's 5th district. This makes Pécs attractive for lifestyle buyers seeking affordable EU-location property.
Is Pécs near the Croatian border a legal issue for non-EU buyers?
Properties within 10 km of Hungary's external Schengen border (including the Croatian border) require OIF permit for non-EU nationals under Government Decree 251/2014. Most Pécs city properties are outside this zone — check the exact coordinates with your lawyer before signing a preliminary contract.
