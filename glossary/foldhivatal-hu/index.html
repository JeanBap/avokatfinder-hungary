Foldnyilvantartasi Hivatal (Land Registry)

Hungary's foldnyilvantartasi hivatal is the land registry office responsible for recording ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances on all real property. Every property transaction must be registered here to take full legal effect.

Property rights in Hungary are publicly recorded in the ingatlan-nyilvantartas (real estate register) maintained by the district land registry offices under the supervision of the National Land Fund Administration. Each property has a unique hrsz (helyrajzi szam — cadastral parcel number) that identifies it in the register.

The register is divided into sheets (lapok): Sheet I describes the land and building; Sheet II records ownership and shares; Sheet III lists encumbrances including mortgages, usufruct rights, easements, and pre-emption rights. All three sheets are publicly accessible and searchable online at the TakarNet system, though full certified extracts require a small fee.

After a deed of sale is signed before a lawyer (Hungary uses lawyers rather than notaries for property transactions), the lawyer must file an application for registration within 30 days. The registry stamps the application date as the priority date — protecting the buyer from any subsequent claims or mortgages.

If there is a mortgage on the property, the seller must provide a bank discharge (torlesztes igazolas) confirming the loan is repaid before the registry will remove the encumbrance. This discharge must be separately filed with a court-approved form.

Foreign nationals may purchase most residential property in Hungary without restriction, but agricultural land purchases by non-EU citizens require special permits and are heavily restricted.

Common Mistake

Watch out: In Hungary, lawyers — not notaries — handle property transfers. Using a lawyer who is not registered with the bar association (Magyar Ugyvedek Kamaraja) means the deed may not be accepted by the registry.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Request a 'tulajdoni lap' (ownership sheet) from TakarNet immediately before signing. Encumbrances can appear overnight and you want the most current version.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the hrsz?

The helyrajzi szam is a unique cadastral number assigned to every plot. Always reference the hrsz rather than the street address when checking the land register, as address records can lag.

Can I check the register myself?

Yes. The TakarNet portal (e-ingatlan.hu) allows public searches. Full certified extracts cost a small administrative fee. Your lawyer should order these as part of due diligence.

How long does registration take?

Standard registration takes 30–60 days. Expedited processing is available for an additional fee if there are no complications.
