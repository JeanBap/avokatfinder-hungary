CSOK Plus (Family Housing Subsidy Plus)

CSOK Plus is Hungary's flagship state housing programme offering subsidised loans and grants to families for purchasing or building homes, with benefits scaling with the number of children.

Launched in 2024 as an evolution of the original CSOK, CSOK Plus provides preferential state-backed mortgage loans at 3% interest — far below market rates — to married couples planning to have children or with existing children. The maximum loan amount scales from HUF 15 million for one child up to HUF 50 million for three or more children.

CSOK Plus is available for both new builds and second-hand properties, removing the previous restriction that the subsidy applied mainly to new construction in rural areas. This expanded scope has made it a major factor in the Budapest and urban property market.

Recipients commit to having a certain number of children within specified timeframes. If the commitment is not met, partial or full repayment of the subsidy is required. A state guarantee system (allami kezesseg) backs the loan, removing the need for private mortgage insurance.

The application is made through participating banks and must be coordinated with the property purchase. Timing is critical: the CSOK Plus contract must be in place before the deed of sale is signed.

CSOK Plus also triggers full stamp duty exemption on the property purchased, making it doubly attractive for eligible families. Income limits and citizenship requirements apply — the applicant must be a Hungarian citizen or a national of an EEA country with Hungarian residency.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Signing the deed before the CSOK Plus contract is officially approved means you cannot apply the subsidy to that transaction. Always confirm the bank's approval in writing first.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Coordinate with your conveyancing lawyer and the CSOK Plus bank simultaneously — the paperwork chains are interdependent and missing a deadline in one can collapse the whole purchase timeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to already have children to apply?

No. CSOK Plus can be applied for by married couples who commit to having children in the future, subject to eligibility rules and repayment obligations if the commitment is not fulfilled.

Can unmarried partners apply?

As of 2024, CSOK Plus requires the applicants to be married. Long-term cohabiting couples do not qualify.

What happens if we separate before meeting the child commitment?

The subsidy becomes partially or fully repayable depending on how much of the commitment was fulfilled. The state mortgage guarantee also lapses and the outstanding loan reverts to standard commercial terms.
